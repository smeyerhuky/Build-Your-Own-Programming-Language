# Pricing

---

## Pricing philosophy

1. **Free tier must be genuinely useful.** Developers will not convert to paid if the free tier feels intentionally crippled. The free tier is our acquisition and trust-building tool.
2. **Pro tier removes friction, not features.** Pro is for power users who want *more* of the same, not for users who need features we've locked away.
3. **Enterprise pricing covers complexity and risk.** Enterprise customers need SSO, audit logs, SLAs, and security reviews. That work is real and justifies a premium.
4. **Academic pricing builds the future pipeline.** Students today are enterprise buyers tomorrow. Price to maximise adoption, not revenue, in the academic channel.

### Academic

**$9/month per student — verified `.edu` email required**

Everything in Pro. No time limit. Auto-renews unless cancelled.

**Faculty accounts:** Free (unlimited) while the faculty member has an active cohort on LangCraft.

**Cohort discounts:**

| Cohort size | Discount | Effective price/student/month |
|---|---|---|
| 10–24 students | 10 % off Pro | $26.10 |
| 25–49 students | 20 % off Pro | $23.20 |
| 50+ students | 40 % off Pro | $17.40 |

*Academic pricing requires annual payment and a signed educational use agreement.*

## Pricing rationale

| Tier | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Free | Developer acquisition; trust-building; referral engine |
| Pro ($29/mo) | Below the "no-approval-needed" threshold for individual engineers; above the "is this real?" threshold |
| Team ($99/mo) | Below $100 keeps it a manager-level decision, not a procurement event |
| Academic ($9/mo per student) | Affordable for students; cohort deal makes it a no-brainer for instructors |
| Enterprise (custom) | Enterprise complexity justifies premium; annual contract ensures commitment |
