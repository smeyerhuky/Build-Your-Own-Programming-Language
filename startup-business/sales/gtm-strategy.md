# Go-to-Market Strategy

---

## Motion: Product-Led Growth (PLG)

LangCraft uses a **product-led growth** motion. The product is the primary acquisition, conversion, and expansion engine. Sales-assisted motions are layered on top for enterprise, not substituted for them.

Why PLG:
- Our audience (developers) is deeply sceptical of marketing. They trust products that work, not salespeople.
- The open-source GitHub repository is already our top-of-funnel asset with proven organic reach.
- PLG aligns incentives: every product investment compounds into growth.

---

## Phase 1 — Land (Q4 2026 — first 6 months)

**Goal:** 3 000 registered users; 150 Pro subscribers; $5K MRR.

### Channels

**1. GitHub (primary)**
- The existing GitHub repository is the core acquisition asset. It already has traction (GitHub stars from the book audience).
- Add a prominent "Try it on LangCraft →" CTA to the README and to every phase of the onboarding bundle.
- Ensure every new commit, release, and issue references LangCraft in a natural way.

**2. Hacker News (Show HN)**
- Launch with a "Show HN: I built an interactive compiler construction platform" post on the day of launch.
- Target: front page for 6+ hours; 200+ upvotes. This single post can drive 1 000+ signups.
- Secondary: "Ask HN" threads on compiler learning; participate authentically with value-first comments.

**3. Reddit — r/ProgrammingLanguages, r/Compilers, r/learnprogramming**
- Share the free tier as a genuine resource. No spammy promotion.
- Engage with existing threads about "how do I learn compiler construction."

**4. Developer newsletters and blogs**
- Pitch to: TLDR.tech, Pointer.io, Morning Brew Tech, Dev.to, Hashnode.
- Clinton publishes a "How I built a complete compiler from scratch" blog post tied to launch.

**5. YouTube**
- Record a 15-minute "build your first lexer in LangCraft" video. Target: 10 000 views in first 90 days.
- Submit to: Fireship, Theo Browne, ThePrimeagen for potential repost/reaction.

### Conversion funnel

```
GitHub visit → README CTA → Sign up (free) → Complete Phase 1 → Upgrade prompt → Pro
```

- Free tier is intentionally designed to be *genuinely useful* (not artificially crippled).
- Upgrade prompt fires when user (a) exhausts daily LLM quota, (b) completes Phase 3, or (c) tries to save a portfolio link.

## Phase 2 — Expand (Q1–Q2 2027)

**Goal:** 10 000 registered users; 500 Pro subscribers; $15K MRR; first 3 academic cohorts; first enterprise pilot.

### Academic channel

- Identify the 20 universities that use the *Build Your Own Programming Language* book.
- Reach out to instructors directly with a free faculty trial account.
- Offer a cohort discount (40 % off) for classes of 10+ students.
- Attend SIGCSE (CS Education conference) and PLDI (Programming Language Design and Implementation).
- Target: 5 university partnerships by end of Q2 2027.

### Enterprise channel

- Monitor GitHub for companies forking the repo or starring it multiple times.
- Identify users who sign up with company email addresses (@companyname.com) — trigger an automated "are you using LangCraft for work?" email.
- Offer a free 30-day enterprise trial to qualified leads.
- First sales hire (Sales Engineer) joins in Q1 2027 to manage the enterprise pipeline.
- Target: 2 signed enterprise pilots by end of Q2 2027.

### Expansion within existing accounts

- Instructor cohorts: once a professor adopts, offer a multi-cohort renewal at a further discount.
- Pro → Team: when a Pro user invites a collaborator, offer Team pricing ($99/mo for 5 seats).
- Community showcase: build a public gallery of student and enterprise projects; drives word-of-mouth.

## Phase 3 — Monetize (Q3–Q4 2027)

**Goal:** $100K ARR; 10 enterprise customers; LangCraft recognised as the reference platform for compiler education.

### Enterprise sales motion

By Q3 2027 the product is enterprise-ready (SSO, audit log, self-hosted option). The sales motion becomes:

1. **Inbound:** Enterprise prospects arrive via the PLG funnel (try free → hit limits → contact sales).
2. **Outbound:** Sales Engineer runs targeted outreach to fintech, cloud infra, AI platform companies known to be building DSLs.
3. **Channel partnerships:** JetBrains marketplace, VS Code marketplace, GitHub Student Pack.
4. **Conference presence:** KubeCon, QCon, AWS re:Invent (targeting enterprise DSL builders).

### University channel maturity

- Partner with 10+ universities for official curriculum adoption.
- Offer a "LangCraft Certified Language Engineer" exam (exam fee: $99) as a revenue stream and credential signal.
- Explore NSF/DOE grant funding for educational technology (Clinton's academic network is the moat here).

## What we will NOT do (Phase 1)

- No outbound sales calls or cold email at this stage.
- No paid advertising (CAC will be too high pre-product-market fit).
- No enterprise-specific features before the enterprise pipeline has 3 qualified leads.
- No conference sponsorship (too expensive; focus budget on product).
