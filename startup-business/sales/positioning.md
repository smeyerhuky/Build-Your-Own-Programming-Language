# Market Positioning

---

## Positioning statement

**For** developers who need to understand or build programming languages,  
**LangCraft** is the interactive compiler construction platform  
**that** takes you from zero to a working compiler in under 10 minutes — with an LLM assistant that knows the curriculum.  
**Unlike** textbooks, video courses, and framework documentation,  
**LangCraft** provides a live, cloud-based sandbox, automated exercise feedback, and enterprise-grade toolchain features — all in one place.

---

## Category creation narrative

LangCraft is not entering an existing category — it is creating one: **interactive compiler construction as a service**.

### The old world

Before LangCraft, building a programming language meant choosing between:

1. **Read a textbook** and figure out environment setup yourself (hours of pain, no feedback loop).
2. **Take a university course** (gated behind enrollment; not available to working professionals).
3. **Use a framework** like ANTLR or LLVM (powerful, but not designed for learning; steep ramp).
4. **Start from scratch** and reinvent every wheel (months of work, no curriculum).

None of these options gives a working developer a *fast, interactive, guided path* from curiosity to a running compiler.

### The new world

LangCraft makes compiler construction as approachable as learning a new JavaScript framework:
- **Browser-based sandbox**: no installation; run a real compiler in 5 clicks.
- **Interactive curriculum**: structured phases, automated exercises, hints on demand.
- **LLM assistant**: ask questions in natural language; get answers grounded in the course content.
- **VS Code integration**: IDE tooling that brings J0 into the professional development environment.
- **Enterprise toolchain**: for teams building DSLs, a production-grade foundation.

---

## Differentiation

### vs. Books and online courses
Books and video courses are passive. LangCraft is interactive. You learn by running code, seeing the output, and fixing errors in real time. The LLM assistant removes the biggest pain point of self-directed learning: *getting stuck with no help*.

### vs. ANTLR
ANTLR is a parser generator — excellent at what it does, but it covers only one phase of a compiler. LangCraft covers the *complete* pipeline. More importantly, LangCraft is designed for learning; ANTLR assumes expertise.

### vs. JetBrains MPS / Xtext
These are powerful enterprise language workbenches, but their learning curves are measured in weeks, not minutes. They are not designed for developers who are new to language construction. LangCraft onboards in 10 minutes and grows with you to enterprise features.

### vs. General cloud IDEs (GitHub Codespaces, VS Code.dev)
These are generic tools. LangCraft is purpose-built for compiler construction — it understands J0, knows the curriculum, and provides exercise scaffolding, automated testing, and a domain-aware LLM. A generic IDE does none of that.
