# Funding Strategy

---

## Philosophy

LangCraft is building a venture-scale business — the TAM is large and the platform has network effects — but we are taking a **capital-efficient path**. We raise only what we need to hit the next milestone. We do not optimise for valuation; we optimise for control, momentum, and investor alignment.

## Pre-seed round ($750K target)

### Instrument

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)** with:
- **Valuation cap:** $5M
- **Discount:** 20 % (standard YC terms)
- **Pro-rata rights:** Yes, for checks ≥ $100K
- **MFN clause:** Yes (all SAFEs get MFN protection)

### Use of funds

| Use | Amount | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Founding engineer salary (12 months) | $180 000 | Month 1 hire |
| Head of DevRel salary (12 months) | $150 000 | Month 2 hire |
| Cloud infrastructure (sandbox + LLM, 18 months) | $120 000 | Ongoing |
| Legal (incorporation, IP assignment, SAFEs) | $30 000 | Month 1 |
| Marketing and launch (blog, video, conference) | $50 000 | Months 1–6 |
| CEO co-founder (deferred salary or equity until close) | $0 cash | Equity comp |
| Clinton living expenses (sabbatical transition) | $70 000 | Months 1–12 |
| Operating reserve | $150 000 | Emergencies |
| **Total** | **$750 000** | |

**Runway:** ~13–15 months to reach $10K MRR milestone.

### Milestones pre-seed funding unlocks

1. Product launched publicly with 3 000 registered users.
2. $5K MRR achieved within 6 months of launch.
3. First academic cohort signed.
4. Enterprise product spec complete (all RFCs in Review or Accepted).

## Seed round ($2–3M target)

### Timing

Target Q4 2027, after:
- $10K+ MRR demonstrated for 3+ consecutive months
- One enterprise pilot signed (proof of enterprise willingness to pay)
- NRR >100 % (evidence of expansion revenue)

### Instrument

**Priced round (Series Seed preferred stock):**
- **Pre-money valuation target:** $12–18M
- **Dilution:** 15–20 %
- **Lead investor:** Prefer developer tools-focused seed fund (Heavybit, Boldstart, Craft Ventures)
- **Round size:** $2.5M (can upsize to $4M if demand)

### Use of seed funds

## Investor profile (pre-seed)

### Ideal pre-seed investor

| Criterion | Preference |
|---|---|
| Check size | $100K–$250K |
| Stage | Pre-seed or seed |
| Domain | Developer tools, edtech, OSS startups |
| Geography | US (but remote-friendly VCs acceptable) |
| Value-add | Academic institution intros OR enterprise DSL buyer intros |
| Time commitment | Monthly check-in; available by email for introductions |

### Investor red flags to avoid

- Investors who want us to pivot away from the open-core model ("why give anything away for free?")
- Investors who don't understand developer tool GTM (PLG is not the same as enterprise SaaS)
- Investors who want board control pre-product-market fit
- Investors who require moving out of New Mexico or pivoting to a different market

### Target investors (research list)

| Fund | Focus | Why them |
|---|---|---|
| Heavybit | Developer tools | Deep expertise in OSS and developer tool GTM |
| Boldstart Ventures | Enterprise, dev tools | Early-stage enterprise focus; strong network |
| Craft Ventures | SaaS, PLG | Experience with PLG → enterprise motions |
| OSS Capital | Open-source startups | Specifically funds open-core businesses |
| Y Combinator | All stages | Access to YC network; academic founder friendly |
| Indie.vc / Calm Company Fund | Bootstrapped/capital-efficient | If we want to stay smaller and profitable |

## Alternatives to external funding

If pre-seed fundraising takes longer than expected, LangCraft can sustain on:

1. **Revenue-based financing:** Once $5K MRR is established, revenue-based financing (Capchase, Pipe) can provide $50–150K against future ARR.
2. **NSF SBIR grants:** Clinton's academic background qualifies LangCraft for NSF Small Business Innovation Research grants (~$275K Phase I). We are applying in Q1 2027.
3. **Packt content deal:** A content licensing deal with Packt to surface book content on the platform could be structured with a revenue share rather than upfront cash.
