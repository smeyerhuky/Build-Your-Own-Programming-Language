## What Is a PR/FAQ and Why Did We Write One?

The PR/FAQ is a discipline invented at Amazon and now widely used by product-led companies. The idea is simple but powerful: **before you build anything, write the press release for the day it ships**. If you can't write a clear, customer-benefit-first press release, you don't yet understand the product well enough to build it.

The format forces two things:

**1. Customer-first language.** A press release is written for a developer reading Hacker News, not for an internal engineering audience. It has to explain what the product does, why it matters, and what specific pain it removes — without acronyms or jargon. If the first draft of your press release sounds like a conference talk abstract, you haven't done the work yet.

**2. Honest self-interrogation.** The FAQ section is the counterbalance. After you've written the optimistic press release, you write the hardest questions a skeptical investor, competitor, or potential customer would ask — and you answer them honestly. The questions that are uncomfortable to answer are the ones that matter most. A FAQ that only has softball questions is useless; the point is to surface the real risks and document your thinking about them now, before you're in the middle of building.

The PR/FAQ is a living document. As the product evolves, the press release and FAQ should be updated. But the original version is preserved in git history as a record of what we believed when we started. The gap between what we wrote in June 2026 and what we ship in June 2027 will be instructive.
